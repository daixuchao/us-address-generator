FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

These answers explain how Global Address Generator works, what the generated data is for, and where the safety boundaries are.

What is this site for?

It is built for product teams, developers, QA teams, designers, and content teams who need address-like examples for forms, demos, CSV samples, screenshots, and localization testing.

Are generated addresses real or deliverable?

No. The generated output is synthetic test data. It may look realistic because it follows common local address patterns, but it is not verified as real, current, or deliverable.

Does the site generate personal data?

The names, phone numbers, and addresses shown by the generators are sample data for testing. They should not be treated as real identities, real phone subscribers, or real customer records.

Can I export generated results?

Yes. The country generators support copying output and exporting CSV files, which is useful for QA test sets, import checks, and demo datasets.

Which countries are available?

The current English generators cover the United States, Japan, and the United Kingdom.

Where can I learn more?

Start with the address format guides, the editorial policy, and the data safety and acceptable use page.
