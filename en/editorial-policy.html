Editorial Policy

Global Address Generator Editorial Team · Last updated July 17, 2026

Global Address Generator publishes guides that help people understand address formats, test-data boundaries, and practical QA workflows for country-specific address fields.

Content scope

Guides focus on address form behavior, postal-code formats, local field labels, CSV export issues, checkout testing, and safe use of synthetic data. The site does not provide legal, financial, identity-verification, or mail-delivery advice.

How guides are written

Each guide is written to answer a specific product or QA question, such as how Address Line 2 works, why ZIP+4 matters in forms, or how UK postcodes should be handled in test data. Examples are designed for education and testing, not for proving that an address exists.

Updates and corrections

When a generator adds a new country, output field, or address variant, related guide links and sitemap entries may be updated. If a page contains an unclear explanation or formatting issue, feedback can be sent through the contact page.

Source priority

  1. Public addressing and postal-code documentation from national postal operators.
  2. Country addressing resources from the Universal Postal Union.
  3. Government or standards-body references for administrative areas and codes.
  4. Original QA workflows that are clearly separated from postal facts.

Human review and AI assistance

Automation may assist with drafting, structural checks, or identifying missing test cases. It is not treated as an authoritative source. Before publication, content is reviewed against the site's generator behavior, practical QA expectations, and primary references. Unsupported claims and rewritten third-party material are removed.

Review includes field order, optional fields, postal-code characters, leading zeros, Unicode, mobile layout, internal links, structured data, copy actions, and CSV output.

Review schedule

Core country-format pages are reviewed at least annually and earlier when generator logic or postal guidance changes. Factual corrections update the visible modified date and sitemap. Reports should include the page URL, disputed field, and a primary reference where available; the normal review target is seven business days.

Advertising and content separation

If advertising is added in the future, ads should not be styled as generated results, form controls, navigation, or download buttons. Editorial content and tool output should remain clearly separate from ad placements.
